Jigsaw (2017) Review

 

·        As a new sadistic game begins, murders start showing up which bear all the same markings as the jigsaw killer’s; leaving the cops to wonder if they’re chasing a copycat or if Jigsaw has somehow returned from the dead.

·        A sequel to the Saw horror/thriller movies

 

 

One of the most disingenuous movies of the year: Jigsaw gutted out all the competence, moral psychology and sleuth the original Saw centered around and instead used the packaging to force-feed us what was essentially an hour and a half long session of middle-school-grade Truth or Dare.

The traps weren’t clever. The victims had all the coordination and common sense of the folks trying to use every day kitchen appliances in late night infomercials. The detective in charge of solving this case was always two steps behind the audience and spent most of the movie chewing gum and smirking at nothing. 

Without exception, the characters were unlikable, bland and the motives were either nonexistent or so forced that it felt like condescension.  

The last act – which was pretty much just a long, horse shit, expository monolog – manically tried to sneak a plot into this otherwise pointless and tedious movie. But no amount of desperate smashing together of these puzzle pieces could have transformed jigsaw into a complete picture. The ending felt less like a well deliberated reveal, and more like a petulant kid trying to sneak in a few more minutes of playtime after mommy already demanded it was time to close the box and put this one to bed.

This is a movie with a built-in fan base. I saw it with a large crowd on opening night and it’s a thriller. As the lights dimmed before the movie, I was worried there would be so much audience participation I’d have to watch this one twice just to write an accurate review; 45 minutes into it, surrounded by a crushing silence, I almost felt the need to personally start blurting out spoilers and opinions, just to boost group morale.

 

PRESCRIPTION:

For a better “rats in a maze” movie: Watch Cube(1997) or The Cabin in the Woods(2012)
