Thor: Ragnarok Review

 

·        Imprisoned without his hammer and facing a gladiator showdown to the death against the Incredible Hulk. Thor races against the clock to free himself and get back to his home world of Asgard, to stop Ragnarok – the prophesized destruction of his entire civilization.  

·        Starring: Chris Hemsworth, Cate Blanchett and Mark Ruffalo

·        Directed by: Taika Waititi (What We Do in the Shadows, Eagle vs Shark, Hunt for the Wilderpeople)

 

If you want lightning in a bottle, look no further than the god of thunder.

Thor score and seven films ago (if you include that two-part short) Chris Hemsworth first donned the blonde wig. None of the previous films were horrible, but his standalones were among Marvel’s weakest and, while the Avengers had a lot to offer, watching a royal demigod struggle to match body counts against his human teammates - who specialize in things like karate and archery – wore thin, in a hurry.  When I first heard they were setting this one on different planets and bringing the Hulk along for the ride, I knew they were on the right track, but I never hoped for something this good.

Like an expert comedian, burning their closing bit by using it as an opener and therefor forcing themselves to come up with stronger material: Ragnarok robbed Thor of his hair, hammer and kingdom before the second act was underway. In doing this, not only were we treated to an exhilarating opening, drenched with a condensed version of what we’ve only got a taste of from this character up until now; but we then got to see a Thor who was outmatched and in uncharted territory.

The story was fantastic and, though, a lot of the scenes were largely improvised, it played as one of the most well thought out movies of its kind. Director Taika Waititi – Who plays the comic relief “Korg” – was exactly what this genre has been missing. Injecting the film with his particular brand of kookiness and leaning into the humor of things, he managed to not only reinvigorate my excitement for Thor and Hulk, but for comic book movies in general. It would be a crime if this showing doesn’t earn the man his pick of projects from now on.

This movie was so complete at every level that the tremendous performances and new characters, which would be the high point of any other film in this class, have to be relegated here to basically an aside. Suffice it to say, if any of a handful of these characters get their own spinoff movie, it’ll be warranted.

If you’re a fan of the genre, you’ll love this one.  If you’re not, honestly, I’d still recommend it. This movie was pure fun. Plain and simple.

 

PRESCRIPTION: 

For another bright fun comic movie: Guardians of the Galaxy.
