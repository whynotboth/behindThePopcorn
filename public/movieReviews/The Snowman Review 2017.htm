The Snowman Review 2017

 

Not even a magical hat could bring this snowman to life.

I suppose, for the sake of orientation, I will note that this movie was a thriller, based on a book, in which Michael Fassbender plays a cop who is either targeting and/or targeted by a serial killer. Aside from that, there’s really nothing worth emphasizing, and it’s in that spirit that I have decided to switch things up a bit. Seeing as how my usual format - in which I begin each review by briefing you on the movie at hand with a list of key facts and helpful details – is rendered null by this debacle of a film, I’m instead going to end this review with a spoiler filled (can you spoil something that was rotten from the onset?) bullet-point list of queries I had rattling around in my head as I left the theater.

I would analyze this movie in more detail, but I fear I’ve already well surpassed the amount of time and effort spent by any other writer regarding this project.

 

*SPOILERS AHEAD*

 

(*Writers not: these aren’t the kind of unanswered questions you might have when leaving a David Lynch movie. Don’t read these and mistake the lack of craftmanship for artistry)

·        What the hell was up with Val Kilmer’s weird voice dubbing? (Hopefully it’s not an insensitive question to ask somehow, but the question is clearly begged)

·        What was the purpose of Chloe Sevigny’s playing twins? Her having a sister, identical or not, added nothing.

·        Was the killer’s identity supposed to be shocking? If so, that was the most lackluster reveal of all times.

·        Why did the killer chop off the limbs? Why was his weapon of choice a shotgun or the wire saw thing? None of that was congruent with his pathology. Shouldn’t he have drowned his victims instead?

·        Why did JK Simmons character have or require a doctor who was secretly his pimp? Why was the doctor of all people pimping to begin with? What was the purpose of his painted toenails.

·        They kept referring to Fassbender’s character as a hero cop. What was it that he was supposed to have done to earn the reverence?

·        Why on Oden’s white earth was Fassbender’s character named Harry Hole?

·        What was with the weird dancing mold exterminator guy?

·        Why were the main male cops on separate timelines both alcoholics for no reason?

·        What was the purpose of the Olympic bid angle?

·        Why would so many really talented actors attach themselves to a script that atrocious?

 

PRESCRIPTION:

For a better movie about cops stuck in a cat and mouse game with a sadistic killer: watch Se7en
