LBJ Review

·        The story of U.S. President Lyndon Baines Johnson. Focused mostly on his role as Vice President to JFK and his drive to be more.

·        Starring: Woody Harrelson, Jennifer Jason Leigh, Richard Jenkins, Jeffrey Donovan and Bill Pullman

·        Directed by Rob Reiner: This Is Spinal Tap, Stand by Me, A few Good Men

 

My trajectory with LBJ the movie was poetically similar to the one this country shared with LBJ the man. As worthy as the movie may have been, it was simply outmatched. I had been wooed by a younger, idealistic, much more handsome one (Thor). I stood outside the crowded ticket booth, feeling excited and optimistic for the Marvel-ous evening that lied ahead, when suddenly - tragedy struck. As I went to swipe my card, after having selected my seat, someone at another window plucked it away from me, in the blink of an eye. The last good seat for Ragnarok, gone. Whether or not this heartless prick at the cementy kiosk acted alone, we may never know. It was a moment of great tragedy, and in that moment, like it or not, there was only one film qualified to take its spot. So, begrudgingly, I took my chair between two grey ponytail sporting gentlemen and braced myself for this new chapter in my night. And I’ve gotta say, I thought it was surprisingly good!

Woody Harrelson was fantastic in the titular role and really gave the character life. Especially when playing against the brilliant Richard Jenkins as Senator Russell. Harrelson’s trademark southern charm was a natural fit for the part, and was made all the more credible by the wholesome (even when making dick metaphors) directing style of Reiner.

What this movie got right was its willingness to explore the little moments that happened in between the headlines and present a portrait without turning the characters into architypes. I also really appreciated the timeline of this movie. By centering the story around the VP years I was able to see a side of this man - and history - that’s usually muddied with the Vietnam of it all. 

Mind you, it’s a movie about LBJ, so don’t go in expecting it to not be that. But I can honestly say, aside from the prosthetics, which at times looked like they would be more at home in Sin City, this was a pretty complete picture. I Laughed out loud several times and walked away with an interest and appreciation for President Johnson that wasn’t there going in.  

PRESCRIPTION:

For other presidential biopics: Watch Lincoln(2012) or for an optimal double feature, pair this with JFK(1991)
