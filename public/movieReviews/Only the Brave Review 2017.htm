Only the Brave Review 2017

 

·        The Granite Mountain Hotshots, a group of elite firefighters put everything on the line to protect a town from a historic wildfire.

·        Based on a true story.

·        Starring: Josh Brolin, Miles Teller, Jeff Bridges, Jennifer Connelly, Taylor Kitsch, Andie MacDowell, and James Badge Dale.

 

 

Going into this movie as a reviewer, I felt like I was looking into the barrel of my worst nightmare. A film about a bunch of Hollywood pretty-boys lifting weights and pretending to be brave heroes while in-between gigs at Marvel. It was not only based on a true story, but a true story about firefighters: So, no matter how badly it sucked, I knew I couldn’t trash it without using a dozen parenthetical qualifiers and lying about having a firefighting uncle to blunt the backlash. It starred Miles Teller – who’s played enough American masculine working-class characters this year to give The Village People grounds for a lawsuit, and it’s just under 2 hours and 15 minutes long. Yikes. But, alas, I am a man of the people and plus it was a slow movie release week, so saw it, I did. And as it turns out, it wasn’t half bad!

The movie could have been shorter, and I wish Andie MacDowell would have gotten a smidgen more screen time but otherwise, it was a decent work on all accounts. They slow played the character development over a long timeline, so you really got to enjoy seeing the relationships form and change and witness the kind of commitment and fortitude required not only in doing the job, but in sharing your life with someone who does. 

I know less than nothing about the real life subject matter, but from what I could tell, it was an honest earnest depiction, and while it’s not exactly an upper of a film, I think it’s worth viewing for anyone who can relate to the story, or anyone willing to take the journey with this crew.

The writing and directing were both impressive, and on both accounts, there was successful zigging where lesser artists would have zagged. However, this movie hinged entirely on the collective sincerity of the large cast, and fortunately, they unanimously delivered; turning what, by all accounts, should have been shameless Oscar-bait into a pretty emotional portrait.

 

PRESCRIPTION:

For another ride with the same pace and tone: Watch Everest(2015) also starring Josh Brolin.

For a less emotionally draining firefighter movie: Watch Backdraft(1991)
