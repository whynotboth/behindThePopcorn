Murder on the Orient Express (2017) Review

·        When his train is marooned by an avalanche, a detective must race to solve a murder before the killer strikes again.

·        Adapted from the novel by Agatha Christie.

·        A remake of the 1974 film by the same name.

·        Written by Michael Green: Alien: Covenant, Logan, Blade Runner 2049.

·        Directed by Kenneth Branagh: Much ado about nothing, Thor, Jack Ryan: Shadow Recruit.

·        Starring: Kenneth Branagh, Penelope Cruz, Willem Dafoe, Johnny Depp, Michelle Pfeiffer.

 

The big engine that couldn’t.

It’s like Clue, mixed with Sherlock Holmes, trapped on a train! Wait, let me rephrase. It’s like being trapped on a train, watching a bunch of people who don’t understand the rules, play a super long game of Clue. 

This film was a literal snoozefest (that’ll come into play later in the review). It wasn’t a complete train wreck, the director/star Branagh had a pretty sweet moustache. Aside from that though, yeah, it was all bad. There was nothing exciting or intriguing offered up here, and any momentum the story initially had going for it got buried in the avalanche along with the train. After that, the bloated running time was spent switching between unearned exposition and force feeding us red herrings like we were protein deficient pescatarians. I’d like to qualify and expand that last statement, but for the sake of not spoiling the mystery reveal, I’ll leave it there. 

Instead of updating this version, Branagh opted to double down on the outdatedness and have his all-star cast play caricature archetypes. (If Hollywood ever sends a live action version of the board game guess who? down the pipeline, this is the man for the job.) Still this eccentric take could have worked if only the brilliant detective would have been written with some brilliance, or if the clues had been even slightly clever and inventive. What we signed up for was to watch a master of deduction piece together subtle but brilliant clues that only seem obvious after he brings them to the forefront for us. What we got was two hours of a truffle pig snorting and splashing around in shit, before, finally, slobbering a mouth full of fungus on our newly polished shoes.

This was a whodunit so tedious, coming home after and trying to figure out which of my step kids forgot to flush felt like an exhilarating game of wits, by contrast.

I don’t remember the original, and I only saw this one in its entirety because as I started to nod off – which is something I’ve never done in a movie before - a tub of popcorn tipped out of the hands of the sleeping moviegoer to my right and spilled over into my seat (a true story!). It was a dark theater, but judging by the rollcall of scattered snoring, at least a half dozen in my theater alone paid to take a nap, and that’s only counting the people with blocked nasal passages.

 

PRESCRIPTION:

For a better movie inspired in part by the original and carrying a lot of the same themes and set up: Watch the 1976 comedy Silver Streak

For a better version: Watch the original Murder on the Orient Express(1974). I can’t say for sure it’s better, but it can’t be worse. Besides, if it sucks, at least you’ll fall asleep in the comfort of your own house, away from rogue tubs of popcorn
